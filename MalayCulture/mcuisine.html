Welcome to Malaysian Malay Cuisine

Take a Deep Dive into the flavorful Cuisines of the Malaysian Malays

Introduction

Traditional Malay cuisine is distinguished by its generous use of spices and coconut milk, which impart a rich, creamy flavor to dishes. Belacan (prawn paste) is another key ingredient, forming the base of sambalâ€”a flavorful condiment made with belacan, chili peppers, onions, and garlic. Lemongrass and galangal are also commonly used in Malay cooking.

Popular Dishes

Asam Pedas

a sour stew of fish (usually mackerel), tamarind, chilli, tomatoes, okra and Vietnamese coriander.

Ayam masak merah

a casserole of chicken pieces in dried chilli sambal.[28] It tends to be a home-cooked dish, so many variations on the recipe exist.

Cincalok

fermented small shrimp or krill. It is usually served as a condiment together with chillies, shallots and lime juice.

Satay

a dish originally from Java and Sumatra in Indonesia, and distributed widely across the Malay Archipelago. It is widely popular and common in Indonesian cuisine with rich variations and recipes. Malay chicken satay closely resembles Madura satay with a rich peanut sauce. In Malaysia, the most popular variant is kajang satay.

Rendang

a spicy meat stew originating from the Minangkabau ethnic group of Indonesia,[33] and adopted by Malay throughout the archipelago. Rendang is traditionally prepared by the Malay community during festive occasions.

Laksa

a spicy noodle soup that consists of thick wheat noodles or rice vermicelli with chicken, prawn or fish, served in spicy soup based on either rich and spicy curry coconut milk or sour asam (tamarind or gelugur) of various types.
